Research Experience

  • Design and implementation of a single phase Quasi-Z-Source Inverter with reduced switching losses
    • Modelled a Quasi-Z-Source Inverter in MATLAB Simulink under three different modulation strategies: The 1P-SM, 2P-SM and the Q-SM (the modified sinusoidal modulation) to determine which of them has the least switching loss
    • Demonstrated that Q-SM is the modulation strategy that posed the least switching loss of 8.39W against 17.69W for 1P-SM and 29.48W for the 2P-SM
  • A comparative analysis of classical three-phase multilevel inverter topologies: Case study: 5-Level
    • Simulated the 5-level Cascaded H-Bridge multilevel inverter (CHB-MLI), Flying capacitor multilevel inverter (FC-MLI), and Neutral Point clamped multilevel inverter (NPC-MLI)
    • Utilized Phase Disposition Pulse Width Modulation for the topologies and FFT tool to perform Total Harmonic Distortion (THD) analysis. A voltage balancing algorithm was implemented in the Neutral Point clamped multilevel inverter (NPC-MLI). CHB-MLI had % THD (Voltage) of 17.28, FC-MLI had 17.35 and NPC-MLI had 17.31. CHB-MLI had an efficiency of 94.99, FC-MLI had 95.01 and NPC-MLI had 72.13. CHB-MLI had a component count of 54, FC-MLI had 70 and NPC-MLI had 88
  • An Examination of Grid Stability with an increasing integration of Inverter-based Generators (IBGs)
    • Led a study in power electronics, meticulously simulating a standard IEEE 14-bus grid inverter-based generator power system using MATLAB Simulink
    • Conducted in-depth analyses, comparing power consumption and voltage stability under both normal and faulty conditions
    • Demonstrated the importance of an Inverter-based generator’s ability to provide reactive power to uphold system stability
  • Improved Pelican Optimization Algorithm with Velocity Effect
    • Developed and implemented a novel exploration enhancement for the Pelican Optimization Algorithm, integrating a wind-effect perturbation method to enhance the exploration capabilities of the POA, improving the accuracy and rate of convergence of optimal values
    • Conducted a comprehensive comparative analysis of the improved algorithm (IPOA) and three counterparts (POA, LTCM-POA, and CIF-POA) across twenty-two benchmark functions. It was also compared with 10 other known algorithms
    • The IPOA achieved benchmark optimum achieved optimal values for 5 out of the 7 unimodal functions, 3 out of the 6 HD multimodal functions and 6 of the 9 FD multimodal functions. Also, the IPOA achieved the best convergence rate in 13 out of the 22 functions
